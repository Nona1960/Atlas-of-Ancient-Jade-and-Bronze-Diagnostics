Founding Principles

Manifesto of Scientific Artefact Diagnostics

Contemporary connoisseurship of ancient Chinese jade and bronze requires a shift from descriptive analysis toward the systematic reconstruction of their long-term material evolution. Under the direction of Professor Nona Dronova, Doctor of Technical Sciences, an integrated methodological platform has been established:

STA

Surface Transformation Analysis

Analysis of the centuries-long mineralogical transformation of jade surfaces under archaeological burial conditions.

TETA

Trace Element Tail Analysis

Analysis of the trace ore-element signatures of ancient bronzes and their relationship to corrosion products (patina).

Both methodologies rest on a single principle: authenticity is determined not by external appearance, but by stable structural and geochemical traces of prolonged interaction between the material and its environment. Together, they form a unified scientific approach to the non-destructive diagnostics of ancient artefacts — uniting mineralogy, geochemistry, and microscopic analysis.
