RO-JADE-009 · Liangzhu Culture Ritual Jade

Jade Bi Disc with Openwork Ornament

Nephrite with layered ochre-brown patina, radial cosmological composition, Liangzhu culture

Nephrite (Tremolite / Jadeite Group) Liangzhu Culture Bi Disc Surface Transformation Analysis (STA)

Macro Detail — Surface and Structure

Section 1

Object Description and Attribution

The object is an archaic ritual bi disc, worked in the tradition of early Chinese sacred geometry associated with the Liangzhu culture. The composition is built on strict radial symmetry: a central circular perforation forms the axis of the structure, around which a ring of rhythmically organised elements unfolds. Around the circumference are twelve segmented zones, each worked with a system of pierced openings of varying form, from rectangular to rounded.

Of particular note are the curved cut-outs of yuekou ("crescent-slit") type, which create an impression of dynamic movement across the surface and a symbolic "breathing" of the structure. The outer ornament is formed by a system of raised, point-like relief nubs, read per the accompanying documentation as star- or energy-markers, reinforcing the object's cosmological significance. The overall composition reflects a developed sense of rhythm, cyclicity, and structural harmony characteristic of the highly developed Liangzhu ritual system.

Section 2 · Surface Transformation Analysis (STA)

Mineralogical and Technological Analysis

Per the accompanying documentation:

  • Mineral: nephrite of the tremolite or jadeite group.
  • Patina: ochre-brown, multi-layered, with deep mineral encrustation formed under prolonged burial conditions.
  • Surface: microcrystalline calcium and siliceous inclusions, characteristic of a moist geochemical environment.
  • Structure: localised zones of flaking of the calcium film and secondary mineralisation.
  • Working: hand polishing and intensive abrasive carving using quartz tools.
  • Perforations: through-drilled, with a pronounced ellipsoidal micro-taper characteristic of ancient drilling technologies predating cylindrical drill bits.

Read against the Atlas's Surface Transformation Analysis (STA) framework, the reported combination of a deep, multi-layered ochre-brown encrustation, localised calcium-film flaking, and secondary mineralisation is consistent with a mineralised secondary surface layer formed over a long burial interval. This is visually consistent with the staining patterns observed in Figs. 3–5 below, which concentrate at drilled perforations and along fine surface fractures rather than lying as a uniform surface wash.

The mineralogical and technological observations above are reported as stated in the accompanying documentation; no SEM or XRF data have been supplied for this specific object, and the observations have not been independently re-examined for this Atlas entry.

Section 3

Cultural and Symbolic Context

Within the Liangzhu cultural system, per the accompanying documentation, the jade bi disc was a key cosmological symbol, associated with conceptions of heaven, order, and sacred authority. The circular form was interpreted as an image of the celestial vault, and the central perforation as an axis of passage between worlds. The pierced elements and radial structure may have functioned as "energy channels," facilitating the circulation of life force (qi). Bi discs were placed in elite burials and accompanied members of the priestly and aristocratic elite, symbolising their connection to the celestial order and to ritual authority.

This section presents historical and cosmological interpretation drawn from the accompanying documentation, not a verified statement about the specific intent of this object's original makers. It is offered as cultural context alongside the material evidence above, not as an independent proof of date or function.
