RO-JADE-004 · Neolithic Ritual Jade

Jade Figure of a Kneeling Bird-Beaked Deity

Olive-green nephrite, Hongshan culture, kneeling anthropomorphic figure with avian features

Nephrite (Tremolite Group) Hongshan Culture Surface Transformation Analysis (STA) Kneeling Figure

Section 1

Object Description and Attribution

The figure depicts a kneeling anthropomorphic being with avian features, carved in the sacred stylistic idiom associated with the Hongshan culture. The composition is frontal and stable, with a compact vertical silhouette. The head is stylised in the form of a bird's beak, suggesting a connection with celestial or aquatic spirits, per the accompanying documentation; the pointed headdress emphasises the figure's ritual status. The pose is kneeling, with the hands resting on the knees — read, per the accompanying documentation, as a gesture of submission, prayer, or ritual mediation. The overall effect combines human and totemic elements characteristic of late Neolithic shamanic sculpture.

Carving is entirely by hand, using ancient abrasive methods; soft grooves and polishing traces are visible, with no indication of modern tooling.

Section 2 · Surface Transformation Analysis (STA)

Scientific Examination

According to the accompanying documentation, scanning electron microscopy (SEM) was carried out on this object (Institute of Steel and Alloys, Moscow), with the following observations reported:

  • A layer of microcrystalline calcite (CaCO₃) with a naturally heterogeneous structure.
  • Secondary manganese–iron inclusions within micropores and depressions.
  • Silicate–carbonate infill consistent with a prolonged burial process.
  • Surface micro-roughness ("dermatoglyphic"-type texture) not reproducible by modern finishing methods.

Read against the Atlas's Surface Transformation Analysis (STA) framework, this combination is consistent with a mineralised secondary surface layer formed over a long burial interval.

The SEM observations above are reported as stated in the accompanying documentation; no SEM imagery for this specific object was supplied alongside the object photographs, and the underlying data have not been independently re-examined for this Atlas entry. They are presented as supporting evidence alongside the macroscopic description, not as a standalone, independently re-verified laboratory result of this page.

Section 3

Cultural and Symbolic Context

The figure belongs to the circle of totemic and shamanic beliefs associated with the Hongshan culture. Per the accompanying documentation:

  • The bird's beak is interpreted as a symbol of a celestial spirit, or of a "rain bird" associated with fertility and the element of water.
  • The kneeling posture reflects ritual submission to the forces of nature or to ancestors.
  • The figure may be regarded as a mediator between the worlds of the living and of spirits, participating in rites addressed to celestial powers.

Comparable images are reported in museum and archaeological collections in China, including collections in Beijing and Liaoning.

This section presents historical and ethnographic interpretation drawn from the accompanying documentation, not a verified statement about the specific intent of this object's original makers. It is offered as cultural context alongside the material evidence above, not as an independent proof of date or function.
